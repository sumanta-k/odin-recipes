Slow Cooker Baby Back Ribs

Description

Slow Cooker Baby Back Ribs are a tender, flavorful, and effortlessly delicious dish where pork ribs are cooked low and slow in a crockpot. The ribs are typically seasoned with a dry rub made of spices like paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, brown sugar, salt, and pepper. This blend infuses the meat with deep, smoky, and sweet flavors.

The slow cooker method ensures the ribs become melt-in-your-mouth tender as they cook for several hours, allowing the flavors to fully penetrate the meat. Once fully cooked, the ribs are slathered with a rich barbecue sauce and finished under a broiler or on a grill to achieve a caramelized, sticky glaze.

These ribs are perfect for gatherings or weeknight meals and are best served with classic sides like coleslaw, baked beans, cornbread, or roasted vegetables. The result is a dish that's both comforting and indulgent, with fall-off-the-bone goodness in every bite.

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds baby back ribs, trimmed
  • salt and ground black pepper, to taste
  • ½ cup water
  • ½ onion, sliced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 (18 ounce) bottle barbeque sauce

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Season ribs with salt and pepper.
  3. Pour 1/2 cup water into the slow cooker, then add ribs. Scatter onion and garlic over top. Cover and cook on Low for 8 hours or High for 4 hours.
  4. When the ribs are almost finished, preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  5. Transfer ribs from the slow cooker to a baking sheet; discard onion and garlic. Coat ribs with barbeque sauce.
  6. Bake ribs in the preheated oven until sauce caramelizes and sticks to meat, 10 to 15 minutes.
  7. Enjoy!
